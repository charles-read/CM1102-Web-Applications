Andreessen's Technological Contribution

Marc Andreessen has contributed a great deal to the development of the computing industry and other uses of technologies. He has produced quality
software and spread ideas that worked well in practice and have allowed business and individuals to work more efficiently. Additionally, he has invested
in companies which also contributes to the expanding world of technology as many of them were only starting up. Marc Andreessen is also associated with
many companies via being a valued member of the Board of directors or advisors for many large companies such as Facebook, Hewlett-Packard and eBay.

Many people consider Marc Andreessen to be the inventor of the graphical internet browser as before he developed Mosaic the world wide
web was mainly accessed through command based platforms which were not very user friendly as commands and a technical background was required to navigate through
pages to get information. Mosaic, later Netscape, allowed users to navigate with mouse clicks, graphical buttons and images. Mosaic was a big push in
the development of human computer interaction on the internet and allowed many businesses and individuals to use the internet without having to study technical documentation.

Loudcloud was founded in 1999 by Andreessen, it was one of the first ideas of using remote servers that run services for businesses on a
national scale. Loudcloud allowed business to rent out these services on a subscription scheme much like how modern cloud file storage subscriptions are used today.
The company was renamed Opsware in 2002 due to internal issues with employees. Opsware pushed forward the idea of having online
systems where businesses don't always need their own local server side services. Additionally, Andreessen partnered
with Ben Horowitz in 2009 to create a private investing company called Andreessen Horowitz. The company focused on investing in startup businesses
which help develop modern technology firms such as Oculus VR, Stack Overflow, Pinterest and BuzzFeed. Overall, as well as personal, business
and development contributions in Netscape and Opsware, Marc Andreessen has motivated and helped evolve many companies and technology firms financially.
