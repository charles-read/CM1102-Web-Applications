Introduction and Personal Life

Marc Andreessen, born in Cedar Falls, Iowa (July 9, 1971), is an American inventor, computer scientist and businessman.
Andreessen co-founded "Mosaic", later renamed to NetScape, which developed one of the most
successful graphical internet browsers of the 1990's. The browser was one of the very first graphical internet
browsers that allowed users to easily navigate through pages with interactive buttons and mouse clicks.
Andreessen Netscape is famous for being a important competitor in the "browser wars" of the 90's where companies like Netscape and Microsoft,
would try to win over the majority of customers/users with new and improved versions of internet browsers with more features.

Born: July 9, 1971
Education: Computer Science, University of Illinois
Net Worth: US$ 600 Million, est.2015
Profession: Inventor, Computer Scientist and Businessman.

While studying at University of Illinois-Champagne, Marc Andreessen developed the Mosaic browser with a small group of programmers in two months.
Mosaic gained large amounts of popularity partly due to it being free software (Around two million people were using it at the time).
After graduating, Andreessen partnered with James Clark (owner of Silicon Graphics Inc) band created the company called Mosaic but changed
it to Netscape due to copyright issues with the University of Illinois. Marc Andreessen, with the same group of programmers who originally
developed the first Mosaic browsers, created a new browser from scratch and named it Netscape which was
released in 1994 with large success which launched Marc Andreessen's career into success.

Over the years, Marc Andreessen has gained experience in different companies as well as investing in start-up companies which has made him a
very successful businessman. Andreessen also founded Loudcloud Inc (renamed Opsware in 1999), which developed systems that could be related to "cloud based"
file services we have today such as Apple's iCloud and Google Drive. Loudcloud provided high speed, software services that could
be rented out to a business, much like personal cloud subscriptions we have today. Additionally, Marc Andreessen is well known
for being a successful investor as many start-up companies he invests are very successful such as Twitter and Qik.
